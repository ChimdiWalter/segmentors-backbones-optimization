Advisor Talking Points — Patch Full Study v2
==============================================
1059 patches from 237 leaves (174 + 63 external 18.7), 7 physics params

ELEVATOR PITCH (30 seconds):
"We extend our selective semi-amortized inference framework from whole leaves
to patches. This companion paper to the full-leaf study preserves spatial
resolution lost by downsampling, reveals within-leaf heterogeneity as a new
biological signal, and introduces patch-to-leaf aggregation as a principled
way to recover leaf-level phenotypes from local estimates."

KEY POINTS FOR DISCUSSION:

1. Companion Paper to Full-Leaf Study
   - Same methodology, same depth, same rigor
   - Same 7 physics parameters, same PDE scorer, same backbones
   - Direct comparison possible at leaf level via aggregation
   - Patch study adds new dimensions not possible at whole-leaf level

2. Preserves Spatial Resolution
   - Whole-leaf processing requires resizing 2000-4000px to 224-518px
   - 224x224 patches maintain native resolution fidelity
   - Architecture-agnostic: works with DINO (384d), ResNet (512d),
     handcrafted (45d) without modification
   - Not about sample count inflation — about preserving biological signal

3. Within-Leaf Heterogeneity Is Novel Finding
   - Multiple oracle theta per leaf reveals parameter non-uniformity
   - Mean within-leaf std(theta) = 1.70
   - New biological evidence: disease severity varies spatially
   - Impossible to observe in the full-leaf study (1 theta per leaf)
   - This is the strongest novel scientific contribution

4. Selective Routing Works at Patch Level
   - 11.1x speedup through selective routing
   - 136 routing policies evaluated via threshold sweep
   - Refinement: direct MAE 5.34 reduced to full refinement MAE 2.11
   - Calibration and risk-coverage validate uncertainty as routing signal

5. Route-Aware Aggregation Outperforms Simple Methods
   - 6 aggregation operators compared systematically
   - Selective mixture: 7.99 leaf-level MAE
   - Other methods: ~10.02 leaf-level MAE
   - Aggregation is a new methodological contribution

ANTICIPATED QUESTIONS:

Q: "Why not just use higher-resolution backbones?"
A: Pretrained backbones have fixed input sizes (224/518px). Fine-tuning on
   full-resolution images requires enormous GPU memory and loses pretrained
   features. Patchification is a principled, architecture-agnostic solution.

Q: "Is the sample size increase (174 to 1059) just inflating statistics?"
A: No. The primary motivation is resolution preservation. The sample size
   increase is a secondary benefit. We maintain parent-leaf-safe splits
   to prevent data leakage.

Q: "How does this compare to MIL approaches?"
A: Our approach is simpler than attention-based MIL but serves a different
   purpose. MIL typically aggregates for classification; we aggregate
   continuous parameter vectors. Attention-based aggregation is future work.

Q: "What about the mean-baseline problem from the full study?"
A: With more patches, learned regressors have more training samples.
   Selective routing remains the key to beating baselines by routing
   uncertain patches to the oracle.
