TITLE
------
"Gradient-Free Stochastic Optimization of Navier-Stokes Active Contours
 for Unsupervised High-Throughput Phenotyping"

AUTHORS
--------
Chimdi Walter Ndubuisi (Student Member, IEEE) and Toni Kazic

ABSTRACT
---------
The paper proposes a fully automated, unsupervised segmentation framework for
plant disease lesions. It treats tuning of Navier-Stokes active contours as a
high-dimensional black-box optimization problem. Key innovations:
- Novel heuristic objective (gradient alignment + chromatic separability + topology)
- Random-restart stochastic hill climbing over 7D parameter space
- Per-image optimization on 174 16-bit leaf images
- Teacher-student distillation into a patch-based UNet
- Trace-based objective-surface analysis showing score-tier-dependent landscapes
- PCA/solid-3D manifold analysis revealing structured parameter organization

KEYWORDS
---------
Active Contours, Navier-Stokes Equations, Stochastic Optimization,
Unsupervised Segmentation, Plant Phenotyping, Computer Vision in Agriculture

SECTION 1: INTRODUCTION
========================================================================
- Precision agriculture needs accurate plant trait quantification
- Lesion morphology is key for crop resistance assessment
- Classical methods (Otsu, K-Means) fail on complex biological imagery
- Deep learning (U-Net, Mask R-CNN, SegFormer, SAM) requires extensive labels
- Physics-based active contours offer unsupervised alternative but are
  sensitive to initialization and hyperparameters
- This paper: self-configuring framework that searches per-image physics
  parameters and yields pseudo-labels for downstream learning

Contributions (4 items):
1. Navier-Stokes diffusion engine for smoothed energy landscapes
2. No-ground-truth objective combining gradient/color/topology terms
3. Gradient-free stochastic optimizer (random-restart + hill climbing)
4. Phenotyping-ready pipeline producing 16-bit overlays/masks

SECTION 4: MATHEMATICAL FRAMEWORK
========================================================================
4.1 Navier-Stokes Diffusion for Edge Enhancement (Eq. 4)
    - Scalar diffusion-reaction PDE update
    - μ as viscosity, (λ+μ)div(∇E) elastic coupling
    - Forcing F from seed vs. image gradient discrepancy (Eq. 5)
    - Edge-gating q = 1[G > T]
    - CFL stability condition: Δt ≤ h²/(4μ)
    - 80 diffusion iterations, normalization to [0,255]
4.2 Active Contour Dynamics (Snakes) (Eq. 6-7)
    - Snake energy: internal (stretching α + curvature β) + external
    - Balloon force γ for outward expansion
    - Multi-lesion handling via thresholded energy map
    - Scale constraints: f_min = 5×10⁻⁴, f_max = 0.45
