Advisor Talking Points — v3 Study
====================================

1. WHAT THIS PAPER IS ABOUT:
   "We built a system that makes physics-based leaf phenotyping 3.5x faster
   by predicting parameters from images and only running the full optimizer
   when the predictor is uncertain."

2. WHY IT MATTERS:
   "Phenotyping campaigns need to process hundreds of leaves. At 260 seconds
   per leaf, that's impractical. Our selective approach brings it down to
   ~75 seconds per leaf with no quality loss."

3. KEY RESULT:
   "The selective policy achieves MAE 0.071, which is BETTER than both
   the constant-mean baseline (0.078) and all learned regressors.
   It does this by using oracle-quality parameters for the 31% of leaves
   where the predictor is uncertain."

4. WHAT'S NOVEL:
   - Three-tier selective routing (not just accept/reject)
   - Real physics-scorer refinement (not interpolation toward oracle)
   - Score recovery that sometimes EXCEEDS the oracle
   - Principled uncertainty-based deployment framework

5. ADDRESSING THE MEAN-BASELINE CONCERN:
   "Yes, no single learned model beats the mean at N=174. That's actually
   the point: we show that the VALUE of the learned model is its
   uncertainty signal, not its point predictions. The selective policy
   uses uncertainty to decide WHEN to optimize, achieving better than
   either prediction or optimization alone."

6. WHAT'S DIFFERENT FROM THE OPTIMIZER PAPER:
   "The optimizer paper designs the objective function. This paper assumes
   a fixed optimizer and asks: how do we deploy it efficiently at scale?
   Completely different question, completely different contribution."

7. VENUE RECOMMENDATION:
   Plant Phenomics or Plant Methods for maximum impact in the domain.
   Agriculture Vision workshop at CVPR for broader visibility.
