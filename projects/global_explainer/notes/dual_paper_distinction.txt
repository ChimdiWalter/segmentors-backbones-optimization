PAPER 1 EXCLUSIVE TERRITORY
"Gradient-Free Stochastic Optimization of Navier-Stokes Active Contours
 for Unsupervised High-Throughput Phenotyping"
================================================================================

The following topics, methods, analyses, and claims belong ONLY to Paper 1.
Paper 2 must not present these as its own contributions.

METHODS:
  - Navier-Stokes active contour formulation and physics parameter space
  - Heuristic objective function design (gradient alignment + chromatic
    separability + topology terms)
  - Random-restart hill climbing optimizer architecture
  - Search strategy: random exploration phase + local refinement phase

ANALYSES:
  - Objective function ablations (drop-one-term experiments)
    * Topology term contribution: +11.12 pts
    * Gradient alignment contribution: +5.09 pts
  - Search strategy ablations (random-only vs full pipeline)
    * Random phase captures ~90% of final score
    * Refinement adds ~10%
  - Trace-based objective surface analysis
    * Score-tier-dependent landscape geometry
    * Sharp peaks for high-score images, flat plateaus for low-score images
  - Parameter-cloud geometry and manifold structure
    * PCA: 2 components capture 46.5% variance
    * t-SNE and UMAP embeddings of solution space
    * Centroid shift: 1.616 standard deviation units
  - k-NN agreement analysis
    * Agreement rate 0.534 vs 0.33 random baseline
    * Parameter shift direction predicts optimization success
  - Distilled UNet student (as secondary consequence of optimization)
    * Mean Dice 0.598, median Dice 0.682

FRAMING:
  - "How to design an unsupervised optimizer for physics-based segmentation"
  - Optimization landscape analysis and understanding
  - Parameter space geometry and structure
  - Method contribution: the optimizer itself

FIGURES & TABLES (exclusive to P1):
  - Objective surface trace plots (representative and full-budget)
  - PCA/t-SNE/UMAP manifold visualizations
  - Ablation bar charts (objective terms and search phases)
  - k-NN agreement plots
  - Optimizer convergence curves
  - 51+ figures in Paper_3/manuscript_final_strengthened/

PAPER 2 EXCLUSIVE TERRITORY
"Selective Semi-Amortized Inference for Runtime-Quality Tradeoffs
 in Physics-Based Lesion Phenotyping"
================================================================================

The following topics, methods, analyses, and claims belong ONLY to Paper 2.
Paper 1 must not present these as its own contributions.

METHODS:
  - Selective routing policy (direct prediction vs short refinement vs full
    optimization)
  - Patchwise aggregation strategy (mean aggregation across patches)
  - Full-leaf vs patchwise comparison framework
  - Semi-amortized inference architecture
  - Uncertainty-based routing decision logic

ANALYSES:
  - Patchwise vs full-leaf parameter estimation
    * Patchwise nMAE 0.906 vs full-leaf 1.154
    * 21.5% improvement, p=0.003
  - Oracle gap quantification
    * 0.327 nMAE irreducible floor from granularity mismatch
  - Selective routing runtime-quality frontier
    * 3.5-4.7x speedup (260s down to 55-74s)
  - Calibration analysis
    * Monotonic uncertainty-error relationship
    * Directionally correct routing decisions
  - Per-parameter routing analysis
    * 5/7 parameters benefit from patchwise
    * 2/7 parameters favor full-leaf
    * Beta: 62.5% improvement with patchwise
  - Cross-collection robustness evaluation
    * 28 external leaves from collection 18.7
  - Failure taxonomy
    * Both good: 29%
    * Full-leaf only: 20%
    * Patchwise only: 20%
    * Both bad: 31%

FRAMING:
  - "How to allocate compute between prediction and optimization at deployment"
  - Runtime-quality tradeoff analysis
  - Deployment-oriented engineering decisions
  - Method contribution: the routing policy and aggregation strategy

FIGURES & TABLES (exclusive to P2):
  - Runtime-quality Pareto frontier plots
  - Risk-coverage curves
  - Per-parameter nMAE comparison tables
  - Failure taxonomy quadrant plots
  - Patchwise vs full-leaf scatter plots

SHARED TERRITORY (MINIMAL — REQUIRES CAREFUL MANAGEMENT)
================================================================================

ITEM 1: Refinement budget table
  In P1: Framed as "how much refinement helps the optimizer" (search ablation).
         Emphasis on convergence behavior and diminishing returns.
  In P2: Framed as "how much refinement budget to allocate per routing tier."
         Emphasis on runtime cost and quality threshold.
  MANAGEMENT: Use different table layouts. P1 shows score vs iteration count.
    P2 shows runtime vs nMAE with routing tier labels. Cite each other.

ITEM 2: Per-parameter difficulty
  In P1: Framed as "some physics parameters are harder to optimize" (optimizer
         landscape insight). Discussed in context of surface geometry.
  In P2: Framed as "some parameters benefit more from patchwise aggregation"
         (routing policy input). Discussed in context of selective routing.
  MANAGEMENT: P1 reports difficulty via optimization convergence metrics.
    P2 reports difficulty via nMAE improvement ratios. Different metrics,
    different tables, complementary interpretations.

ITEM 3: The 174-image dataset
  In P1: Primary evaluation set for optimizer convergence and ablations.
  In P2: Training/evaluation set for routing policy and aggregation study.
  MANAGEMENT: Both papers describe the dataset. Use consistent numbers but
    different levels of detail. P1 emphasizes image-level properties relevant
    to optimization. P2 emphasizes patch-level statistics relevant to
    aggregation.

ITEM 4: The Navier-Stokes active contour model
  In P1: Central object of study. Full mathematical formulation presented.
  In P2: Treated as a black-box optimizer that the routing policy wraps around.
         Mathematical details referenced via citation to P1.
  MANAGEMENT: P2 includes a brief model summary (1-2 paragraphs) and cites P1
    for full details. P2 should NOT re-derive the physics equations.

================================================================================
ONE-SENTENCE DISTINCTION TEST
================================================================================

If a reviewer asks "how is this different from your other paper?":

  Paper 1 answer: "Paper 1 designs and analyzes the optimizer; Paper 2 decides
  when to use it."

  Paper 2 answer: "Paper 1 shows the optimizer works; Paper 2 shows how to
  deploy it efficiently by routing between direct prediction and optimization."
