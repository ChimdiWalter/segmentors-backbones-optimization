## Central Contribution

This paper treats the tuning of Navier-Stokes-driven active contours as a
per-image black-box optimization problem. A heuristic objective function
(gradient alignment + Lab chromatic separability + topological priors)
replaces ground-truth supervision. Random-restart stochastic hill climbing
finds per-image physics parameters (viscosity, tension, rigidity, balloon
force, energy threshold) over a bounded search space.

Key results on 174 high-resolution 16-bit leaf images:
- Fully automated unsupervised segmentation of necrotic cores and chlorotic halos
- Teacher-student distillation into a patch-based UNet (median Dice 0.68)
- Trace-based objective surfaces reveal score-tier-dependent landscape geometry
- PCA analysis of the solution manifold shows structured, low-dimensional
  parameter organization (46.5% variance in 2 PCs, centroid shift 1.616 s.u.)
- k-NN agreement (0.534 vs 0.33 baseline) shows shift direction predicts
  optimization success
