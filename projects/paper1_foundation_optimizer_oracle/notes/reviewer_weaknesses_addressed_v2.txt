Paper 1 v2 — Reviewer Weaknesses Addressed
==========================================
File: manuscript/main_paper1_v2.tex
Compiled PDF: pdf/paper1_v2_final.pdf
Date: 2026-03-31

WEAKNESS 1 — ABSTRACT: CO-EQUAL FRAMING OF OPTIMIZER AND DISTILLATION
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Type: Rhetorical / scope
Problem: The v1 abstract presented distillation as a co-equal contribution alongside
  the optimizer and the no-GT objective. A reviewer focused on the core contribution
  would correctly note that distillation is a consequence, not the central claim.
Fix applied: Rewrote abstract to lead with the optimizer and no-GT objective as primary
  contribution. Distillation is mentioned explicitly as "a secondary consequence" in the
  final sentence. All key quantitative results (ablation drops: -34% and -75%, kNN 0.534,
  centroid shift 1.616) moved to the abstract for immediate evidence anchoring.

WEAKNESS 2 — RECOMMENDATION-STYLE WRITING IN RESULTS/EXPERIMENTS
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Type: Rhetorical (future-tense / academic hedging)
Problem: Multiple "We recommend..." statements throughout the manuscript:
  - Baselines section: "We recommend comparing against..."
  - Metrics section: "In addition to Dice/IoU, we recommend..."
  - Qualitative Results section: "We recommend organizing qualitative results by..."
  - Discussion: "recommend a small manually annotated subset to adjudicate..."
Fix applied:
  - Baselines: Rewritten as "For completeness, classical approaches serve as natural
    baselines... The present evaluation focuses on the no-GT objective and distillation
    metrics; external annotated comparison is deferred to follow-up work."
  - Metrics: Changed to "we report:" with specific figures/tables cited.
  - Qualitative Results: Changed to "Figure X organizes qualitative results across the
    Dice spectrum, spanning..."
  - Discussion: Changed to active description with note that adjudication "is reserved
    as a natural extension of this work."

WEAKNESS 3 — INFORMAL PLACEHOLDER LANGUAGE IN MULTI-STAGE SECTION
------------------------------------------------------------------
Type: Rhetorical / professionalism
Problem: Section 7 (Multi-Stage Inference) opened with:
  "Your later code indicates a practical phenomenon: broad lesions may contain inner
  oscillating/necrotic substructures."
  This is informal placeholder language from drafting, not publication-ready text.
Fix applied: Replaced with:
  "Broad lesions frequently contain inner oscillating or necrotic substructures that
  are biologically distinct from the outer chlorotic halo. We address this with a
  two-stage nested inference strategy:"

WEAKNESS 4 — RESULTS SECTION ORDERING (DISTILLATION BEFORE ABLATIONS)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Type: Structural
Problem: The Results section in v1 placed Teacher-Student Distillation results (Dice
  numbers, threshold sweep, montages) BEFORE the ablation studies and trace analysis.
  This made it appear as if distillation was the primary result.
Fix applied: Restructured Results section ordering:
  1. Optimizer Performance and Claim-to-Evidence Summary (new, with Table III)
  2. Teacher-Student Distillation Results (relabeled as secondary, with framing:
     "As a secondary application of the optimizer pseudo-labels...")
  3. Parameter Correlations with Morphology
  4. Ablation Studies (objective and search) — now clearly primary evidence
  5. Trace-Based Objective-Surface Analysis
  6. Parameter-Cloud Geometry and Manifold Structure
  7. Qualitative Results and Failure Taxonomy

WEAKNESS 5 — NO CLAIM-TO-EVIDENCE SUMMARY TABLE
------------------------------------------------
Type: Structural / evidential
Problem: A reviewer could not easily match each paper claim to the specific experiment
  or table supporting it, making the paper feel diffuse.
Fix applied: Added Table III "Claim-to-evidence mapping" at the start of Results.
  Seven primary claims mapped to specific figures/tables and quantitative evidence.

WEAKNESS 6 — MANIFOLD/GEOMETRY OVERSTATEMENT
--------------------------------------------
Type: Evidential / rhetorical
Problem: Discussion language could be read as claiming the system "learns the governing
  physics." Specifically: "This is consistent with the physics of the PDE pipeline"
  could be interpreted as a stronger mechanistic claim than the data support.
Fix applied: Replaced with explicit disclaimer:
  "We emphasize that this is a statement about the geometry of the optimizer's output
  space, not a claim that the method learns the governing physics from images."
  Reframed the energy threshold dominance finding as operational rather than physical.

WEAKNESS 7 — PARAMETER CORRELATIONS SECTION (RECOMMENDATION LANGUAGE)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Type: Rhetorical
Problem: "We recommend analyzing correlations between theta* and morphology:" followed
  by bullet points read as future-work suggestions.
Fix applied: Changed to direct description: "The optimized parameters exhibit interpretable
  correlations with lesion morphology. High-threshold, high-inflation solutions correspond
  to discrete necrotic spots; [etc.]" — treating these as completed observations.
