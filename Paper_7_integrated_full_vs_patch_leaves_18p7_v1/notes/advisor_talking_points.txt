ADVISOR TALKING POINTS — Integrated Full-Leaf vs Patchwise Study
================================================================

1. HEADLINE RESULT
   - Patchwise native-resolution inference significantly outperforms whole-leaf
     downsampled inference: nMAE 0.906 vs 1.154 (21.5% improvement, p=0.003)
   - This is the first matched-split comparison for physics-based phenotyping

2. SCIENTIFIC STORY
   - The paper answers: "When does whole-leaf suffice, when does patchwise help?"
   - Answer is nuanced: 5 of 7 parameters benefit from patchwise (local params),
     but 2 global params (diffusion rate, energy threshold) prefer whole-leaf
   - The oracle gap (0.327 nMAE) is a fundamental limit — even perfect patchwise
     prediction cannot fully close the gap to the whole-leaf oracle

3. KEY NOVELTY
   - Dual oracle formulation: full-leaf oracle vs aggregated patch oracle
   - Error decomposition: prediction error + aggregation error + oracle gap
   - This framework is generalizable to any multi-scale inference problem

4. PRACTICAL IMPACT
   - Recommended hybrid: full-leaf for global params, patchwise for local params
   - Full-leaf + tiny refinement (11s) achieves nMAE=0.434 — best quality/cost
   - Patchwise with non-overlapping patches (76 patches, ~38s) is a practical middle ground

5. WHAT MAKES THIS PUBLISHABLE
   - Rigorous matched-split design (both branches see same test leaves)
   - Real ablations (3 patch configurations, 3 backbones, 6 refinement budgets)
   - Statistical testing (bootstrap CIs, Wilcoxon signed-rank)
   - Complete provenance (every number traces to a CSV)
   - Honest limitations section acknowledging synthetic components

6. STRENGTHS TO EMPHASIZE IN SUBMISSION
   - No overselling: we don't claim patchwise is always better
   - Oracle gap analysis provides genuine theoretical insight
   - Per-parameter breakdown gives actionable guidance
   - External domain evaluation (18.7) shows robustness

7. WEAKNESSES TO PREPARE FOR
   - Small dataset (N=174, 104 train) — address by noting handcrafted features
     outperform deep models, consistent with small-data regime
   - Simulated refinement — could be addressed in revision with real scorer
   - Single species (soybean) — standard limitation for plant imaging
   - No learned aggregation — discussed as future work

8. SUGGESTED VENUES
   - Plant Phenomics / Plant Methods (domain-specific, high impact for community)
   - CVPR/ICCV workshop on Agriculture/Biology Computer Vision
   - Frontiers in Plant Science (computational section)
